Cleaning Performance & Dual-Nozzle Effectiveness

The fundamental question with any bidet attachment is simple: does it actually get you clean? The NEO 120 delivers here with impressive consistency. The dual-nozzle system features two separate spray heads that retract when not in use and extend only when activated, protecting them from contamination. The rear wash nozzle provides a focused, moderately powerful stream that adjusts via the pressure control dial from gentle to quite vigorous. During testing, even at lower pressure settings, the cleaning effectiveness was excellent, thoroughly removing residue without harsh blast that cheaper attachments produce. The feminine wash nozzle sits at a slightly different angle and provides a softer, wider spray pattern that testers reported as comfortable and effective. This dual-nozzle design is particularly valuable for households where multiple people use the bidet, maintaining better hygiene separation than single-nozzle designs.

Build Quality & Long-Term Durability

For a budget-friendly attachment, the NEO 120 punches well above its weight class in construction quality. The main body is crafted from chrome-plated ABS plastic, which resists corrosion and maintains appearance in humid bathroom environments. The internal components include brass and stainless steel parts in critical pressure points, ensuring longevity. The control lever operates smoothly with satisfying mechanical feedback, and after months of daily use across test households, we saw no degradation in performance or drips. The T-valve adapter connecting to your toilet's water supply line is solid brass with rubber gaskets creating leak-proof seals. Nearly 100,000 customer reviews provide real-world validation of this durability, with countless users reporting years of trouble-free daily use.

Installation Experience & Daily Use

Installation follows the standard bidet attachment process, but LUXE includes particularly clear illustrated instructions making the job approachable for those with minimal DIY experience. The process takes approximately 15-20 minutes for first-timers. You'll turn off your toilet's water supply, disconnect the supply hose, install the included T-valve, reconnect everything, and mount the bidet attachment between your toilet bowl and existing seat. Daily operation is refreshingly simple. After using the toilet, you remain seated and turn the control knob to your preferred pressure level. The nozzle extends automatically, delivers your wash, and retracts when you return the dial to off position. The entire process takes 20-30 seconds. The control knob is positioned on the right side within easy reach while seated, with clear markings and distinct stopping points. One thoughtful design detail: the attachment's low profile means your toilet seat sits nearly flush, maintaining normal sitting height and comfort.
