How to Choose the Right Robotic Pool Cleaner

Pool Type & Size

Match the cleaner to your pool type (in-ground vs above-ground) and size. Larger pools need models with longer cables and extended runtime.

Cleaning Coverage

Consider what areas need cleaning: floor only, or also walls and waterline. Premium models offer complete coverage including surface skimming.

Filtration System

Fine filters capture small particles, while large debris baskets handle leaves. Some models offer multiple filter options for different debris types.

Smart Features

Wi-Fi connectivity, app control, and scheduling add convenience. Decide if these premium features justify the extra cost for your needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do robotic pool cleaners last?

With proper maintenance, quality robotic pool cleaners typically last 3-5 years. Premium models with better build quality can last even longer with regular filter cleaning and proper storage.

Are cordless models as effective as corded ones?

Modern cordless models have improved significantly and can be just as effective for most pools. However, they typically have shorter runtime and may require more frequent charging for larger pools.

Can robotic cleaners handle all types of debris?

Most models handle common debris well, but capabilities vary. Fine sand requires fine filtration, while large leaves need spacious debris baskets. Check each model's specifications for your specific needs.
